# Research Protocol

Use this protocol to move assumptions to evidence. Keep the process lightweight but defensible.

## Table of contents

1. Research objective
2. Evidence quality ladder
3. Core research tracks
4. Evidence log format
5. Confidence update rules

## 1) Research objective

For each low-confidence or high-impact assumption:

1. Write the exact question to answer.
2. Define what decision it affects.
3. Define what evidence would change the decision.

## 2) Evidence quality ladder

Prefer higher tiers first.

1. Primary data: customer interviews, transaction data, pipeline data.
2. Authoritative data: government statistics, regulator publications, public filings.
3. Industry sources: credible analyst reports, trade bodies, major benchmark studies.
4. Secondary commentary: blogs, social commentary, opinion pieces.

When using lower-tier evidence, mark confidence as lower unless corroborated.

## 3) Core research tracks

## 3.1 Demand and customer reality

- Validate pain severity and willingness to pay.
- Gather evidence from interviews, conversion data, and demand proxies.
- Output: prioritized customer segments + decision-relevant insights.

## 3.2 Market sizing and structure

- Build TAM/SAM/SOM from transparent assumptions.
- Capture geographic and segment boundaries.
- Output: sizing model with assumptions and confidence level.

## 3.3 Competition and alternatives

- Identify direct competitors, substitutes, and incumbent workflows.
- Compare value proposition, pricing, and channel strategy.
- Output: positioning map and defensibility hypotheses.

## 3.4 Channel and acquisition economics

- Estimate channel cost, speed, scalability, and risk.
- Identify leading indicators for channel viability.
- Output: channel portfolio with near-term testing sequence.

## 3.5 Unit economics and financial viability

- Estimate CAC, gross margin, payback period, churn, and LTV logic.
- Build base/upside/downside scenarios.
- Output: scenario table with constraints and breakpoints.

## 3.6 Legal and operational constraints

- Identify regulatory, tax, contractual, and compliance constraints.
- Identify execution bottlenecks and capability gaps.
- Output: risk register with mitigation ownership.

## 4) Evidence log format

Use this for every source:

- `Question:` assumption being tested
- `Source:` publisher or system of record
- `Date:` publication date and retrieval date
- `Scope:` geography, segment, and timeframe
- `Finding:` concise summary
- `Impact:` what decision this changes
- `Confidence delta:` increase/decrease/no-change

## 5) Confidence update rules

- Increase confidence only when at least two independent sources align.
- Downgrade confidence when evidence is stale, conflicting, or non-comparable.
- Mark assumptions as `validated`, `partially validated`, or `unvalidated`.
- Do not hide uncertainty; convert uncertainty into explicit next actions.
