## Summary

Professor Stefano Albrecht introduces Multi-Agent Reinforcement Learning (MARL),
detailing its theoretical foundations and practical applications in robotics and
gaming. He highlights resources from the University of Edinburgh, including the
EPyMARL codebase and the first comprehensive MARL textbook published by MIT
Press.

## Key Points

- MARL focuses on training multiple autonomous agents to interact and solve
  tasks in shared dynamic environments.
- The University of Edinburgh's School of Informatics is a historic leader in AI
  research, hosting one of the first three AI labs in the world.
- Practical applications for MARL include warehouse automation, Level 4
  self-driving vehicles, and automated trading.
- EPyMARL is a research-grade codebase designed to standardize multi-agent
  reinforcement learning implementations.
- The MIT Press MARL textbook is presented as a self-contained resource covering
  Game Theory, Deep Learning, and RL.

## AI Concepts

Multi-Agent Reinforcement Learning (MARL), Deep Reinforcement Learning, Nash
Equilibrium, Game Theory, Population-Based Training, Partial Observability,
Counterfactual Information, Imitation Learning
