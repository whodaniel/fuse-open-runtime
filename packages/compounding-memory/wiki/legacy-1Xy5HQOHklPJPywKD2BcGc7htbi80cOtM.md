## Summary

The video outlines essential strategies for Social Security Disability Insurance
(SSDI) and Supplemental Security Income (SSI) recipients to retain their
benefits during future reviews. The speaker emphasizes the importance of
continuous medical documentation, obtaining specific functional capacity
letters, and maintaining privacy to avoid potential fraud investigations.

## Key Points

- Provide medical evidence showing no medical improvement or worsening of
  symptoms to satisfy Continuing Disability Reviews (CDRs).
- Consistently obtain updated Residual Functional Capacity (RFC) letters from
  physicians to document specific vocational limitations.
- Maintain regular medical appointments to demonstrate continuity of treatment,
  even if the condition is perceived as untreatable.
- Document 'crisis' medical treatments, such as emergency room visits, to prove
  a lack of medical stability.
- Protect social media privacy by using pseudonyms and avoiding posts that could
  be misinterpreted as physical capability.
- Limit personal 'enemies' and be cautious in social circles to prevent
  malicious reports to the Office of Inspector General (OIG).
- Avoid overexerting yourself to 'impress' others, as these actions can be used
  as evidence of fraud or improvement.
- Advise veterans with caregivers to ensure their spouses/helpers are certified
  and maintain daily logs of assistance.
